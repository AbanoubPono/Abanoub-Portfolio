About Us

The world around us is becoming increasingly complex, especially in the field of technology. Keeping up with these changes is essential—understanding them, using them, and even developing new solutions. Technology offers limitless possibilities to improve individual lives and society as a whole. As students at El-Gazera Higher Institute for Computer Science and Information Systems, we are passionate about innovation and problem-solving. Our team has embarked on an exciting project: building a remotely controlled robot. This robot consists of a mobile vehicle and a robotic arm, both of which can be controlled through a custom-designed application. About the Project You might be wondering—what is the purpose of this project? This robot offers unlimited possibilities for performing various tasks.

For example:

If you want to check whether your pet has enough food, you can use the robot to inspect the food supply and, if necessary, use the robotic arm to provide more food. The robot can also be used to handle hazardous materials, such as radioactive substances, since it is not affected by them, unlike humans. In agriculture, the robot can measure soil moisture levels and determine whether plants need watering. One of the key features of this robot is that it can be programmed to operate automatically, reducing the need for human intervention. The applications of this technology extend to industries such as manufacturing, food production, and many others. Robotics plays a crucial role in improving efficiency, reducing costs, and minimizing human error. Our project is just one example of how robots can make life easier, safer, and more efficient. In this guide, we will cover: Understanding the robot and its components. How to connect and operate the robot. Programming the robot. The robot’s mechanical functions. Improvements made to the robot. The team behind this project. We will also discuss the broader impact of robotics and how these innovations continue to shape our daily lives.

Second: The Arm Movement Section: The arm contains a set of servos that control its movement.
Servo01_Right: Responsible for moving the arm's base to the right.
Servo01_Left: Responsible for moving the arm's base to the left.
Servo02_Up: Responsible for moving the first joint upward.
